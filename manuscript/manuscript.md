# SwarmLineage-OT v1.2

Native moscot infers an OT pseudo-lineage map from time-series single-cell snapshots. SwarmLineage-OT converts that map into an executable finite-agent virtual-cell system and evaluates the resulting dynamics by teacher fidelity, branch-order-parameter robustness and external time-series support.

The retained result remains focused: under the native teacher and E1 MouseGastrulationData support, the strongest computational hypothesis is a branch-nucleation order-parameter signature interpreted as transient condensation-before-divergence. The primary model is M5_ot_swarm, selected for teacher fidelity and mechanism evidence rather than architectural completeness.

v1.2 adds a Parisi-inspired local-rule audit. Topological k-neighbour, metric-radius, random, label-restricted and mixed neighbour rules were compared on internal, E1, L2 and E2 datasets. The result is deliberately conservative: topological neighbours can reproduce the branch signature, but metric rules also reproduce it and random controls are not clean in all datasets. Therefore the local-rule conclusion is dataset-specific, not a standalone topological-neighbour mechanism.

The best fitted topological k is 2, and the 5-9 neighbour range is not uniquely supported. Susceptibility analysis supports only a weak high-susceptibility branch-window diagnostic; finite-size scaling is not established. A minimal pairwise topological model gives acceptable prototype-level prediction of separation direction, but it remains a diagnostic model. Local perturbation propagation is localized in silico and has no experimental status.

L2 clone-aware CellTag validation remains failed under the current operationalization: condensation exposure did not support clone-level branch splitting. Diffusion remains an encoded control-law recovery. Birth/death, memory and CCI remain unsupported and are excluded from the main claim.

Current manuscript language must remain computational: the project supports a native-OT-guided branch-nucleation hypothesis with external time-series support, not clone-resolved or experimental confirmation.
