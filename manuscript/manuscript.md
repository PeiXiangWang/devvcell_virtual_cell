# SwarmLineage-OT: A Developmental Branch-Window Order-Parameter Framework

SwarmLineage-OT converts native OT-inferred developmental pseudo-lineage maps into executable finite-agent virtual-cell dynamics. The retained manuscript story is now a developmental branch-window order-parameter taxonomy and evidence-boundary framework. Transient condensation-before-divergence remains the strongest observed signature in internal native and E1 MouseGastrulationData, but the broader contribution is the ability to classify transition windows and explicitly report where proposed mechanisms fail.

The strongest evidence remains internal native moscot and E1 MouseGastrulationData. A final independent embryoid-body and spatial/time-series sprint did not upgrade the external atlas above weak support: GSE154572 lacks curated lineage labels and STDS0000074/GSE123187 is not branch-window-ready in the inspected cell-level form. Therefore the manuscript should state that broader cross-system generalization remains unresolved.

Clone fate prediction, topological-neighbour specificity, swarm-required causality, birth/death, memory, validated CCI mechanism, GRN causal control and diffusion as an independent discovery are excluded from the main claim.

We further audited whether branch windows become more stable in GRN/regulon space. Native pySCENIC/GRNBoost2 and CellOracle-style tools were not available in the current environment, but the project reuses PathwayFinder's local OmniPath CollecTRI/DoRothEA TF-target priors, with TF-target co-expression used only as a fallback when prior targets are absent. This recovered candidate developmental TF programs but still did not produce clean internal/E1 negative-control support for a generalized regulatory branch-window mechanism. The GRN layer is therefore retained as a regulatory audit and candidate-generation module, not as evidence that a causal regulatory mechanism has been established.

The breakthrough synthesis tested ten candidate upgrades: branch-window taxonomy, GRN transition taxonomy, known developmental program recovery, uncertainty atlas, agent-versus-teacher value, failure-boundary atlas, detector calibration, regulatory perturbation prioritization, spatial-validation requirements and final story selection. No new biological mechanism reached strong evidence. The most defensible story is an OT-guided virtual-cell framework that provides branch-window taxonomy, interpretable rollout order parameters and a rigorous map of unsupported claims.

A new communication-niche search reuses PathwayFinder's local OmniPath intercell prior to define a local sender--receiver field from neighbor transmitter expression and receiver/membrane competence. The full LR perturbation/rerollout mechanism remains unsupported, but communication-niche priming is now the strongest CCI-aligned candidate annotation: E1 MouseGastrulationData and GSE154572 EB reach acceptable computational support, and the morphogen-patterning module shows positive branch-window activation in internal native, E1 and EB. This is a candidate extracellular-niche layer for future spatial or perturbation validation, not validated ligand--receptor signalling.

A stricter Communication-Niche v2 analysis filtered OmniPath to extracellular sender--receiver pairs and split the broad morphogen signal into FGF, WNT, BMP, TGF/NODAL/ACTIVIN, SHH, Notch/Delta, ECM/guidance and chemokine/growth-factor niches. This removed most generic intracellular pairs and downgraded the family-level result to weak: FGF is the strongest cross-dataset candidate, with acceptable support in E1 and weak same-direction support in E5 zebrafish; WNT is acceptable in the EB dataset. Thus, the broad communication-niche field remains more robust than any single morphogen family, while FGF/WNT become concrete spatial/perturbation validation targets.
