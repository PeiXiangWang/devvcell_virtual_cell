# Editorial Assessment

The v1.3 package is stronger as a rigorous mechanism audit than as a positive mechanism paper. Native moscot teacher construction and E1 time-series support keep the branch-nucleation hypothesis worth retaining, but the attempted local-rule explanation is not strong.

Topological-neighbour rules reproduce parts of the branch signature, yet metric-radius rules also reproduce the signature and random controls are not clean enough. This prevents a topological-specific claim. The minimal pairwise model is acceptable as a diagnostic, but it does not establish mechanism.

L2 clone-aware validation remains a failed test for the primary condensation-to-clone-splitting claim. Alternative exposure definitions show confounding rather than rescue. Diffusion remains an encoded control-law recovery, and birth/death, memory and CCI remain unsupported.

The best manuscript posture is a restrained computational story: SwarmLineage-OT yields a branch-nucleation order-parameter hypothesis with external time-series support, while local-rule origin, swarm necessity and clone-level support remain unresolved.
